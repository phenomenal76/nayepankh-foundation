What We Do

Four Pillars of Change

Our programmes tackle the most urgent needs of underserved communities — one family, one child, one woman at a time.

📚

Free Education

We run learning centres that give children from low-income households access to quality tutoring, school supplies, and mentorship — planting seeds for lifelong growth.

Learn more →
🍱

Food Distribution

Regular meal drives and community kitchens ensure that no one in our reach goes to bed hungry. Nutritious meals are distributed across urban slums and rural pockets.

Learn more →
🌸

Women's Hygiene Awareness

Through workshops and hygiene kit drives, we break taboos around menstrual health and sanitation — empowering women and adolescent girls with knowledge and dignity.

👗

Clothing Drives

Seasonal clothing collections and distribution camps help families maintain warmth and dignity throughout the year — especially children and the elderly in winter months.

Our Story

Born from a Belief that Change Begins Locally

NayePankh Foundation was founded by a group of young changemakers who believed that compassion, when organised, can transform lives. Starting with a single tutoring session in a Delhi slum, we have grown into a movement that spans education, nutrition, health, and dignity.

Grassroots Approach

We work directly within communities, led by local volunteers who understand the ground reality.

Zero Overhead Promise

Every rupee donated goes to programme delivery — transparent and accountable at every step.

Dignity First

All our interventions are designed to preserve the dignity and agency of those we serve.

Real Impact

Stories of Change

Behind every number is a life transformed. Here are a few of the many journeys that keep us going.

🧒
Education

Riya's First Report Card

Riya, 9, from East Delhi had never attended school regularly. After six months at our learning centre, she handed her mother her first report card — with a 92% score.

👩
Hygiene

Sunita Breaks the Silence

After attending our menstrual health workshop, Sunita became a peer educator in her village, reaching over 120 women who had never discussed hygiene openly before.

👴
Clothing

Warm Winters for Ramesh

Ramesh, 72, spent last winter without a proper blanket. Our clothing drive reached his neighbourhood just in time — and he now volunteers at every collection camp.
